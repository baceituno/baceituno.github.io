About me

I'm an Electronics Engineering undergraduate student at Simon Bolivar University (USB). My research interests are in the areas of Robot Locomotion and Optimal Motion Planning. I'm specially interested in finding optimal and robust solutions from non-convex motion planning problems , particularly involving robots moving through uncertain and challenging environments.

I work as an Undergraduate Researcher at the USB Mechatronics Research Group under the advise of professor G. Fernandez, where I'm developing optimization-based robust locomotion planning strategies for multilegged robots. I'm currently collaborating with the Dynamic Legged Systems Laboratory (DLS) at the Italian Institute of Technology (IIT), developing a model-based optimization planning approach for HyQ.

In the past, I also worked along prof. Jose Cappelletto as part of the UW Vision project developing an Underwater Robotics Perception Toolbox for large scale underwater 3D reconstructions. Project done in collaboration with the USB Experimental Ecology Laboratory.

Research

Dynamically Aware Simultaneous Contact and Non-Gaited Walking Motion Planning via Mixed Integer Convex Optimization
Ongoing

I'm currently collaborating with Hongkai Dai and the IIT-DLS in the development of a Multilegged Locomotion strategy based on Mixed-Integer Convex Optimization under the advise of professor G. Fernandez. Our goal is to formulate the walking motion and contact sequence planning problem as a single convex optimization problem. We are approaching the problem with Mixed-Integer Convex Optimization and Robust Optimization, in order to integrate rough terrain and angular dynamics in the convex formulation. Our goal is to generate: footstep plans in complex 3D environments, gait sequences that adapt to the unevenness of the terrain, and body motions that are consistent with the multi-contact dynamics of the system within a single optimization problem.

Robust Non-Gaited Walking Motion Planning for Multilegged Robots on Rough Terrain via Convex Optimization
October 2016 - February 2017

I collaborated with Hongkai Dai in the development of a Robust Multilegged Locomotion Planning Framework under the advise of professors J. Grieco and G. Fernandez. Our proposed strategy is able to generate both: free-gaits that adapt to the unevenness of the terrain and body motions that maximize the Contact Wrench Cone (CWC) stability margin. Generating robust motions even on very challenging terrains where classic Zero Moment Point (ZMP) planners would fail.

Our approach divides the problem in two stages: a set of Mixed-Integer constraints within an optimization-based footstep planner to generate free-gaits and footstep plans, and a Center of Mass (CoM) and Angular Momentum trajectory planner that maximizes the robustness of the motion. This second stage treats the CoM position as an uncertain variable in a ellipsoidal set, therefore turning the bilinear relations in the problem into second-order cones.

Generalized Multilegged Footstep Planning via Mixed-Integer Optimization
Summer 2016

I worked in the development of a generalized footstep planner based on Mixed-Integer Convex Programming (MICP) for multilegged walking robots. Our approach treats the non-convex variables associated to obstacle avoidance, body orientation and plan length with Mixed-Integer constraints over convex segments, which makes the problem convex. Our implementation also considers kinematic reachability constraints based on the geometry of the robot, therefore making the planner generalizable to any multilegged robot, regardless of the number of legs. Our implementation is capable of generating average length plans on fractions of a second, using on-board processing technology.

Before this project, the entirety of successful optimization-based footstep planners were developed for humanoid robots and none existed for multilegged robots, mainly due to the non-convex nature of the leg reachable space and the geometry of the robot. This had the drawback that most of the existing multilegged planners were based on discrete searches, which often don't consider the continuous elements of a 3D environment. This work represents, to our knowledge, the first successful implementation of an optimization-based footstep planner for multilegged robots.
