About me

I'm an Electronics Engineering undergraduate student at Universidad Simón Bolívar (USB). My research interests broadly encompass Robot Locomotion and Optimal Motion Planning. Specially, in finding optimal solutions from non-convex motion planning problems when involving robots moving through uncertain and challenging environments with novel tools such as Mixed Integer Programming and Robust Optimization.

I'm currently working as an Undergraduate Researcher in the USB Mechatronics Research Group along professors J. Grieco, G. Fernandez and J. Cappelletto, developing an optimization-based robust locomotion planning strategy for multilegged robots.

In the past I also worked as part of the UW Vision project developing a Underwater Robotics Perception Toolbox under the advice of professor Jose Cappelletto for large scale underwater 3D reconstructions, project done along the USB Experimental Ecology Laboratory.
