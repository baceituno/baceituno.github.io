About me

I'm an Electronics Engineering undergraduate student at Simon Bolivar University (USB). My research interests are in the areas of Robot Manipulation and Locomotion. I'm specially interested in finding model-based solutions to complex planning and control problems, particularly when interacting with the robot's environment and exploiting the dynamic structure of the problem allows for agile and dexterous behavior.

I work as an Undergraduate Researcher at the USB Mechatronics Research Group under the advice of professors G. Fernandez and J. Grieco, where I'm developing model-based optimization strategies for multi-contact locomotion and dexterous manipulation.

In the past, I collaborated with the Dynamic Legged Systems Laboratory (DLS) at the Italian Institute of Technology (IIT), where I developed a model-based optimization planning approach for HyQ. I also worked along prof. Jose Cappelletto as part of the UW Vision project, developing an Underwater Robotics Perception Toolbox for large-scale underwater 3D reconstructions. Project done in collaboration with the USB Experimental Ecology Laboratory.
